TOPIC

This course provides a basic introduction to content production, including pre-production, actual production, and post-production.We use smartphone cameras for recording video and external audio, and PremierePro and Audition for editing

GOAL

You will be introduced to basic professional concepts in content production, and you will also use the skills you have learnt in previous topics to redesign a company website

PROJECT 1 - PASSIONSITE

PROCESS

Interview

For the first part of this project we had to interview someone with a passion as a group of two, and create a video production to display on a website. I chose to interview a friend who was passionate about nail art

Video Production

The recording of video was done using an iPhone 16, capturing outgoing audio with on another iPhone simultaneously. We went on to use Adobe Premiere Pro as our editing program, learning how to cut, crop, edit and insert text and royalty free music to create 1 minute interview video.

Lottiefile

We had to make a Lottiefile, a “gif” type of element, for the first time using Adobe After Effects. I created a text that floated across a set image with a 5 second duration

CODING & RESULTS

Key Elements

This was a one page site we had to make so by using my past experience of creating a header, footer and main body elements, I was able to capture all the elemets i needed of the video, lottiefile and picture collage.

Learnings & Solutions

The hardest part of this process was to find the correct ratio for the grids, both large and small, so that no image or text would overlap so I will take with me in my future projects that to have a set ratio/margin on the borders of my site and to practice more on grid structuring so that I am prepared to tackle them.

The general experience I also gained from this project was how to approach creating a website from both a designers angle and a developers angle as there were quite a few times throughout this project where I had to to sacrafice

PROJECT 2 - COMPANY SITE

PROCESS

Figma

For this group project we worked together as 4 people to create a redesign of a real life brands website. Together, we used a figjam board for the research process, finding our target audience, making moodboards, styletiles, solution sketches, tests on our target audience and developing our wireframes in order to get to the final step of coding our website

Solution sketch

I was assigned to code the “contact us” page, so I went through the process of creating a “crazy 8” sketch where you draw 8 squares on one paper and have 60 seconds for each square to sketch concept ideas of your site. This led me and my group to decide which sketch fit our the best and from that I created the solution sketch as displayed.

CODING & RESULTS

Contact Form

One of the new areas of code I had to tackle was a contact form where I used a template that guided me through the process in HTML and CSS. I learned it was a lot easier than i expected and by editing within CSS, I could make it match to our styletile

Map

I also learned through this coding process how to insert a map onto a website through google maps which was just by using the given embedded “iframe” code that google maps has available.

Learnings & Solutions

Working on a website redesign for a company as part of a group project taught me the importance of collaboration and communication in the design process. I gained valuable experience in brainstorming ideas, dividing tasks, and combining different perspectives to create a cohesive final product. This project also helped me improve my skills in design, coding, and problem-solving while learning how to balance creativity with meeting client needs.
